Empowering Parents: A Guide to Digital Privacy for Families

Exploring the vital topic of digital privacy and its relevance in today's connected world.





Lesson 1.1: The Importance of Digital Privacy

Imagine a world without privacy. Everything you do, everything your family shares, everything you say or search online is out there in the open for everyone and anyone to see. We would be be living in a world filled with risks and consequences.

Delving into the risks and consequences of inadequate online privacy, you will learn why digital privacy is so essential in safeguarding your family's well-being and data.



Lesson 1.2: Understanding Privacy Terminology

To fully understand digital privacy, you must first familiarise yourselves with some key terminology and concepts.

Use the images below to discover the relevant terminologies.

Personal Data

Personal data is defined by any information which is related to an identified person. E.g., telephone number, credit card, and address.

- GDPR

Data Breaches

A personal data breach means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal data. This includes accidental or deliberate causes. It also means that a breach is more than just about losing personal data.

- Information Commissioner's Office

Tracking & Cookies

Tracking and cookies are tools that are commonly used in websites and services to monitor users' behaviour and prefernces.
