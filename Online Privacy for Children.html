Lesson 2.1: Children's Privacy Rights

Children are among the most vulnerable individuals in terms of data privacy and users of the internet, their naive nature and lack of awareness about the many risks the internet presents, make them easy targets for exploitation and data misuse. As parents or guardians, it is essential to understand how laws and regulations protect our children online, and what procedures we can take to ascertain their safety and privacy. So, safeguarding children's privacy rights is not just a matter of legal compliance but also an ethical responsibility, it becomes crucial for their safety and well - being.

Laws and regulations around the globe recognise this and aim to safeguard and prioritise children's privacy rights. Let's have a look at some of these laws.



Laws & Regulations

Key Principles

Two prominent regulations that safeguard children's online privacy are COPPA and GDPR.

The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), in the United States, prohibits unfair or deceptive acts or practices in connection with the collection, use, and/or disclosure of personal information from and about children on the internet. COPPA imposes certain requirements on operators of websites or online services directed to children under 13 years of age. -Federal Trade Commission

The General Data Protection Act (GDPR), in the European Union, states the processing of the personal data of a child shall be lawful where the child is atleast 16 years old. Where the child is below the age of 16 years, such processing shall be lawful only if and to the extent that consent is given or authorised by the holder of parental responsibility over the child. -GDPR

  • Consent: Children and their parents must be informed about data collection practises and must provide consent.


  • Data Minimisation: Only collect the necessary data for specific purposes and avoid excessive data collection.


  • Parental Control: Parents should have the ability to control their child's personal information and their online activities.


  • Security Measures: Implement robust security measures to protect children's data from unauthorised access or breaches.




Best practices for Parents & Carers

  • Educate Your Children: Teach your children about the importance of privacy and online safety.


  • Use Parental Controls: Explore and use the different parental control tools offered by the digital platforms and devices that your children use, to limit their exposure to inappropriate content.


  • Monitor Online Activites: Regularly monitor your child's online activities and interactions.


  • Regularly Review Privacy Policies: Read and understand the privacy policies of the websites, apps, and devices your child uses.


  • Encourage Open Communication: Ensure your child feels comfortable discussing their online experiences and concerns.




Lesson 2.2: Safe Browsing for Kids


In this lesson we will be exploring the strategies for ensuring safe and age-appropriate online content for our children. With the internet being a vast and sometimes unpredictable space, it is essential to equip ourselves with the tools and knowledge to protect our children while they browse.

Safe browsing for kids is essential for protecting them from harmful content, online predators, and other digital risks. By implementing effective parental controls and teaching safe browsing habits, we can mitigate these risks and provide our children with a safer online environment.



Parental Control Tools

Across all the platforms and websites your child uses, there is a large pool of parental control tools and filters that many parents do not use or are not aware of. These tools have been created to help parents with managing their child's online experience. Thes tools can allow you as parents to block inappropriate websites, set time limits, and monitor your child's online activity. Popular parental control tools include:

  • Content Filters: Filter out inappropriate content based on predefined or cutsom settings.


  • Usage Controls: Set time limits for and restrict access during certain hours.


  • Safe Search Settings: Enable safe search settings on search engines to filter out explicit content.


  • Monitoring Apps: Use apps to track your child's online activity and, receive notifications for any concerning behaviour.




Age-Appropriate Content

Educating Children About Online Safety

It is essential for children who browse the web, to access content that is safe and age-appropriate. Most apps have age ratings or recommendations, these can help parents make informed decisions about the content their child can access. Ensure your child is only accessing secure websites, that have a valid https certificate, and avoid clicking on unfamiliar links or adverts.

Teaching your child about online safety is just as important, if not even more important than implementing parental controls. As parents you have the vital role of educating your child about the many risks associated with sharing personal information online, interacting with strangers, and accessing inappropriate content.
